## The problem

> A customer walks onto your forecourt at 8pm. The showroom is shut. They're stood in front
> of a car they might buy — and all they can see is a price.
>
> They can't see the mileage, the MOT history, the forty photos or the finance options.
> So they walk away. And you never even knew they were there.

## The solution

CarTag Studio prints a **window label for every car**, carrying a large **QR code**.

1. The customer **scans it with their phone**
2. They land **straight on that car's page on your website**
3. They see **your photos, your specification, your finance calculator**

Day or night. Showroom open or shut.

And it handles the tedious part for you — **it watches your website, and the moment you
list a new car or change a price, the label prints itself.**

---

## What it does

### Labels that print themselves

- **Add a car to your website** → its label is printing before you've reached the printer
- **Change a price** → a new label prints with the new figure
- **Sell a car** → it removes it and tidies up after itself

No typing. No copy-pasting. No *"I'll do it later"*.

### It reads your website for you

- Paste a car's link and it **fills in the name, price, mileage and registration** itself
- Works on **any dealer website** — including ones built with JavaScript, where ordinary tools see nothing
- Knows the difference between the **car's real price** and the *"£154 per month"* finance figure next to it

### It watches your stock

| It sees | It does |
|---|---|
| A new car listed | Prints its label |
| A price change | Prints a fresh label with the new price |
| A car sold and removed | Clears it from your records |

It finds your stock page on its own and checks it every few minutes. Nothing to remember,
nothing to maintain.

### It brings people to your website

- Every scan is a **real visit to a car page on your own site**
- From someone **stood in front of that car**, at the moment they're interested
- They land on the pages that turn a browser into an enquiry
- Optional **scan tracking** shows the visits in **Google Analytics**, so you can see exactly how many people scanned your labels

### Built for the forecourt

- **A price you can read across the car park** — it scales itself to fill the label
- **Six label designs** — Classic · Bold · Banner · Framed · Minimal · Compact
- **Your dealership name and tagline** on every label
- **Any label size** — 4×6, 3×2, Brother 62 mm, or a custom size you type in
- **Any printer** — Munbyn, Zebra, Brother, DYMO, or an ordinary office printer

### Every label you've ever printed

- **Searchable** by registration, car name or price
- **Reprint with one click** when a label gets tatty or peels off in the rain
- **Print in bulk** — one label, or your whole forecourt

### It simply runs

Starts with the computer and sits quietly in the tray. You'll forget it's there — until you
notice the labels are always right.

## Common questions

<details>
<summary><b>Will it work with my website?</b></summary><br>

Almost certainly. It reads the same page your customers see — so if a person can read the
price, so can CarTag Studio. It has been tested against a range of dealer platforms,
including sites that build their pages entirely with JavaScript.
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Will it work with my printer?</b></summary><br>

Any printer your computer can already print to — Munbyn, Zebra, Brother, DYMO, or an
ordinary office printer. The label size is up to you.
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Could it print a hundred labels by mistake?</b></summary><br>

No. On the first run it takes a quiet snapshot of your existing stock and prints
<b>nothing</b>. After that it only prints what is genuinely <b>new</b> or <b>changed</b>,
and there is a safety limit on top of that.
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>What happens if the internet drops out?</b></summary><br>

Printing carries on as normal. It picks your website back up when the connection returns.
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Can I still print a one-off label myself?</b></summary><br>

Yes. Paste the car's link, check the details, print.
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Will it slow my computer down?</b></summary><br>

No. It sits in the tray using very little, and wakes every few minutes to check your
stock page.
</details>
